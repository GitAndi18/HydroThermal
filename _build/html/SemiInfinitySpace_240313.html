1D-Heat Transport Equation#

Heat transport in homogeneous materials is described by the heat transport equation. This is also known as Fourier’s 2nd law, named after the French researcher Fourier who worked intensively on the phenomenon of “heat”. The equation in one dimension is

\[ \rho \, c_p \, \dot{\vartheta}(x, t) = \lambda \, \vartheta^{\prime\prime}(x, t) \]

In semi-infinite space, a cosine-shaped excitation at the surface results in the following solution (the derivation is done in the lecture) for the oscillating case.

\[ \vartheta(x, t) = \Delta \vartheta_0 \, \text{e}^{-\frac{x}{\sigma}} \, \text{cos}(\omega t - \frac{x}{\sigma}) \]

with the surface flucuations \(\vartheta_0(t) = \Delta \vartheta_0 \, \text{cos}(\omega t)\) and the penetration depth \(\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{T}{\pi}\,a}\). \(a\) is the thermal diffusity defined as \(a = \frac{\lambda}{\rho c_p}\) and \(T\) is the period of the harmonic fluctuations.

The following graph shows characteristic temperature curves in the ground for an annual period.

Show code cell source Hide code cell source 
T = 3600*24*365
w = 2*np.pi / T
Nt = 11
Nx = 50
x_max = 10
t = np.linspace(0, T, Nt)
x = np.linspace(0, x_max, Nx)
theta = np.zeros((Nt, Nx))
dtheta_0   = 10
theta_mean = 10
lam    = 1
rhocp  = 1500*1000
a      = lam / rhocp
sigma  = (T/np.pi * a)**0.5

for n, tt in enumerate(t):
    theta[n, :] = theta_mean + dtheta_0 * np.exp(-x / sigma) * np.cos(w*tt + x / sigma)

#%matplotlib ipympl
fig = plt.figure(figsize=(13, 5))
for n in range(Nt):
    plt.plot(x, theta[n, :], label=str(n))
plt.legend(loc='center right')
plt.title('Yearly Temperature Fluctuations in the Ground')
plt.xlabel('$x$ / m')    
plt.ylabel(r'$\vartheta$ /°C')
plt.grid()
plt.show()
